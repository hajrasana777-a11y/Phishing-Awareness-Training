Stay Safe from Phishing Attacks

Learn how to recognize phishing emails, fake websites, and social engineering attacks before they steal their information.

What is Phishing

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers pretend to be trusted organizations to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, banking details, and personal information.

Types of Phishing

✉️ Email Phishing

Fake emails that steal passwords and personal information.

📱Smishing

Fradulent SMS messages asking you to click melicious liks.

🎯Spear Phishing

Fraudulent SMS messages asking you to click malicious links.

How to recognize Phishing Emails

📧Check the sender

Be careful with emails that pressure you to act immediately.

⚠️Urgent Messages

Be careful with emails that pressure you to act immediately.

📝Spelling Errors

Many phishing emails contain grammar and spelling mistakes.

🔗Suspicious Links

Never click unknown links or download unexpected attachments.

How to identify Fake Websites

  • Always check if website uses HTTPS.
  • Look carefully at the website URL.
  • Watch for spelling mistakes in the domain name.
  • Avoid websites with poor design or suspicious pop-ups.
  • Do not enter personsal information or untrusted websites.

Example:
✅ www.paypal.com
❌ www.class="paypa1.com"

Social Engineering Tactics

Social engineering is a technique used by attackers to manipulate people into revealing confidential information.

  • Fear _ "Your account will be blocked."
  • Urgency _ "Act now before it's too late."
  • Trust _ "Pretending to be a bank or company."
  • Curiosity _ "See who viewed your profile."
  • Rewards _ "Congratulations! You won a prize."

Best Practices

🔒Use Strong Passwors

Create Unique and complex passwords for every account.

🛡️Enable 2FA

Turnon Two-Factor Authentication for extra security.

🔄Keep Software Update

Install updates regularly to fix security vulnurabilities.

👀 Verify Before Clicking

Always check links, email, and websites before entering personal information.

Real-World Phishing Examples

🎣Fake PayPal Email Scam

Cybercriminals sent fake paypal emails asking users to verify their accounts. The email contained a fake login page that stole usernames and passwords. Many users lost access to their accounts because they entered their credentials on the fake website.

Lesson Learned:

  • Always verify the sender before clicking links.
  • Be careful when an email asks for account permissions.
  • Think before you click.
